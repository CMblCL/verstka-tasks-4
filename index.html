Verdict for Old Plug Hat is the smallest Given in Local Courts.

Heber Col­erran's old plug hat was worth just 23 cents, accord­ing to Jus­tice Pierce's ruiing yes­ter­day, and though the for­mer val­ued the top­per at $8. he went away triumphant because the small­est ver­dict giv­en in a Los An­ge­les court al-o carried costs against the defen­dant, Morris T. Berzer, dyer and cleaner. West Sev­enth street.

I wish I'd nev­er seen the oli hat. exclaimed the lat­ter. Coherman said he was gol­ing to be married and wanted the thing ironed in a hurry. My as­sistant told him It was worth­less and ought to go into the ash barrel. Cole­man said he must have it and promised to pay $1.50. He didn't come back for it at the time he agreed so, af­ter a long pe­riod an actor came in and said he wanted an old plug hat. we offered him Cole­man's at $1.50. He kicked hard at paying that price, but as he had to have one he finaliy gave in and we came out even. Then Cole­man turned up and de­manded his hat.

I nev­er said I was go­ing to get married. tes­ti­fied Cole­man. it cost me $8 to get a new plug hat, when the old one was good enough for my purposes and I think I should get that $8.

He went away with the ver­dict tor 25 cents and looked happy.

STUDENT SEEKS TO UPSET WILL.

says He is one of Heirs to VAST FortUNE.

Young Aus­trian Cheva­li­er Entered at col­lege of Medicine of Uni­versity of South­ern California Fights for Share of Eight Million Dollars Left by Rel­ative to Breslau.

Quietly pursu­ing his stud­ies as a student of medicine here is a young Aus­trian, pos­sible help to $8.000.000, a commander and cheva­li­er of a Portaguese or­der of nobility, and the rel­ative of a fa­mous general who took part in the conflicts be­tween Germany and Aus­tria which resulted in the for­mer obtain­ing Breslau.

Alexander S. Balla is the name under which the chevail­er is entered at the col­lege of medicine. U.S.C. He rooms at № 210 North Hill street.

Gen. E. Frankel, my grandmoth­er's cousin, has just died at Breslau. said he yes­ter­day. leav­ing his fortune of 40.000.000 marks, or $8.000.000 to the hos­pitals there. It is my in­tention to fight the will as I consid­er it most unjust that my grandmoth­er, moth­er, my­self and broth­er should be overlookol in the dis­tri­bution of the estate.

My grandmoth­er, Babette Frankel, is still alive. She is 85 years old, and, Gen. Frankel was her cousin. He was n Aus­trian, but he stayed in Breslau af­ter it was cap­tured by the Germans. He was born there, he lived there all his life, he fought against German occap­ation, and he died there, and in dying he left ev­ery­thing to the city, which is most unfair tơ us, his four helrs. The sole rel­atives are my grandmoth­er, moth­er, broth­er and my­self, and we should share in the estate with Breslau.

The estate consists of land, houses and mon­ey. Gen. Frankel loaned large surns to the city of Breslau and he left a huge sum in cash. The mon­ey lent to the city and expended princirally in building hos­pitals and I suppose he felt it would be a nice thing to wipe out the debt by his legacy. But he forgot his rel­atives and we have re­solved to con­test the will.

Do you think Gen. Frankel desinher­ited you because of any squabble? he was asked.

My moth­er's fam­ily had no quarrel with him. was the reply.

Balla came to the United States two years ago from Vi­enna, where his moth­er and broth­er re­side. He lived in San Francisco for a few months, but at the time of the earth­quake and fire became to Los An­ge­les.

If I get my share of this fortune I shal rema in this beautiful South­ern California. he remarked.

The ti­tles which he holds where confarred on him by the late King Car­los of Portu­gal. My fam­ily reptered politacal ser­vices to Portu­gal. said he and the King who has just been as­sas­sinated rec­ognized these by hon­or­ing me.
